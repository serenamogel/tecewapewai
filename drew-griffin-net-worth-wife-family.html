Meet His Wife And Family

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, September 3, 2024

Drew Griffin, CNN’s Senior Investigative Correspondent, is no more. He is survived by his wife, kids, and grandkids.

Drew Griffin was a reporter on the American news channel CNN. He joined the network in 2004. 

He had been an investigative reporter for CBS 2 News for ten years.

Griffin had covered several critical issues, such as the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina, the looting by some New Orleans Police officers, and nursing home deaths after Katrina.

He was an award-winning news personality with accolades, including Emmys, Peabodys, and Murrows.

What Was Drew Griffin Net Worth Before Death?

The internet lacks official/ verified sources of Drew Griffin’s net worth before his death. Since he was a highly experienced reporter, he was likely doing financially well.

Griffin started his career as a reporter/cameraman for WICD-TV and KIRO-TV and joined CBS 2 News in January 1994.

After working as an investigative reporter for CBS 2 News in Los Angeles for a decade, he moved to CNN in May 2004.

Griffin served on Cable News Network until his death in December 2022. It means he spent over 18 years with the network.

As a part of CNN, Griffin covered various important news like the aftermath of Hurricane Katrina in September 2005 and nursing home deaths after Katrina.

Moreover, his reports about the looting by a few New Orleans Police officers led to a Police investigation.

Griffin was honored for his exemplary work. He won a 2005 business and financial reporting Emmy Award for his reporting work on a fault in Ford Motor Company vehicles that caused them to catch Fire.

A year later, he earned his second Emmy for his CNN Presents documentary How to Rob a Bank.

He had a hattrick of Emmys, with his third coming in 2007 for Hidden Spending, an Anderson Cooper 360° segment highlighting congressional spending. 

Likewise, Griffin received the 2015 Peabody Award for Crisis at the VA and his fourth Emmy for Trump University Fraud in 2017.

Meet CNN Reporter Wife Margot Griffin And Family

A Chicago native, Drew Griffin is survived by his wife, Margot Griffin, three children, and two grandchildren.

When not working, his family says, he loved to travel with his wife, play the trumpet, or play golf with friends.

His children’s names were inspired by jazz greats – sons Louis and Miles Griffin and daughter Ele Gast.

Poppy Marlow, his CNN colleague, took to Twitter to express condolence for the Griffin family.

“To his wife Margot and their three children, I am so sorry for your loss,” she posted.

Likewise, Erica Hill wrote, “Heartbreaking loss this morning. Sending love to his family.”

Drew Griffin Died From Cancer In December 2022

Drew Griffin, CNN’s award-winning Senior Investigative Correspondent, died Saturday (December 17) after a long battle with Cancer. He was 60.

His family confirmed the unfortunate news. His colleagues praised Griffin’s solid work ethic, who had kept his illness private from most of his co-workers.

Griffin had been reporting until the day he passed. “His death is a devastating loss to CNN and our entire profession,” CNN’s CEO Chris Licht said in a note to staff.

He described Griffin as a highly acclaimed investigative Journalist with a considerable impact. “He embodied the mission of this organization in every way,” Licht added.
