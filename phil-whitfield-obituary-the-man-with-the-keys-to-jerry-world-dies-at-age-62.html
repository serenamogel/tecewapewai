The man with the keys to 'Jerry World,' dies at age 62

Posted by Aldo Pusey on Thursday, August 22, 2024

The Dallas Cowboys and AT&T Stadium community mourn the loss of Phil Whitfield, a revered figure known for his remarkable ability to open any door within the stadium. On July 26, at the age of 62, Phil Whitfield was found unresponsive in his Dallas home, leaving behind a legacy that will forever be cherished.

Phil Whitfield, born on July 29, 1960, in Dallas, began his journey with the Dallas Cowboys three decades ago as a security guard at the old Texas Stadium. Over time, his dedication and loyalty to the organization earned him the position of AT&T Stadium and Art Ambassador in 2009, a role he held until his passing. As we mourn the loss of Phil Whitfield, it is with heavy hearts that we remember the remarkable contributions he made to the Dallas Cowboys community. An Inseparable Bond with the Cowboys

Phil Whitfield’s bond with the Dallas Cowboys was more than just a job; it was an inseparable connection that defined his life. His passion for the team ran deep, fueling his every action and decision throughout his three-decade journey with the organization. But it was not just his dedication to the team that made him stand out; it was his profound relationship with team owner Jerry Jones and his wife Gene that truly set him apart.

To Phil, the Jones family was more than just employers; they were like family to him. This sentiment was echoed by his brother, Roosevelt Whitfield, who witnessed firsthand the profound love and affection Phil had for the team and how the Jones family reciprocated that sentiment in kind.

The close bond between Phil and the Jones family extended beyond the realm of business. They celebrated victories together, shared moments of joy, and stood together through challenges and triumphs. Their mutual respect and admiration created an atmosphere of warmth and camaraderie that permeated throughout the organization, affectionately known as “Jerry World.”

Beyond his role as a security guard at the old Texas Stadium, Phil became an integral part of the Cowboys’ identity. His journey led him to the position of AT&T Stadium and Art Ambassador, a role he embraced with pride and enthusiasm. Phil’s ability to unlock doors metaphorically reflected the welcoming spirit he embodied and the genuine care he showed for everyone he encountered.

Phil Whitfield’s impact was not limited to the Cowboys community alone. His passion for art made him a prominent figure among art enthusiasts, as he used his platform to promote and appreciate artistic expression within the stadium’s walls.

As we remember Phil Whitfield and the legacy he leaves behind, we recognize that his devotion to the Dallas Cowboys and the Jones family went beyond the call of duty. He personified the values of dedication, loyalty, and love for one’s work, inspiring all who had the privilege of knowing him. Phil’s spirit will forever resonate in the hearts of those who shared in his journey, and his memory will continue to shape the spirit of the Dallas Cowboys for generations to come.

A Visionary in Art Appreciation

Beyond his contributions to security and management, Phil Whitfield played a crucial role in promoting art at AT&T Stadium. He conducted public art tours, showcasing captivating murals, pictures, and sculptures to the countless fans visiting the stadium each year. Phil’s appreciation for art aligned perfectly with the Jones family’s vision of creating a stadium that catered to a broader audience, extending beyond sports enthusiasts.

A Life of Passion

A proud Dallas native, Whitfield attended Dallas ISD’s Roosevelt High School during his formative years. Throughout his life, he believed that passion was the common thread uniting all artists. As he once remarked, “Do what you love to do, and you’ll never work a day in your life.”
